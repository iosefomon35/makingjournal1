# Week 01 Data Portrait

## Documentation 

Since I pick up my phone often during the day without giving it much thought, I decided to keep track of how I feel each time I do so. My phone is practically a habit in my everyday life, and I wanted to know the reasons behind doing this. Instead of simply assuming that I use my phone for fun or a chat, I wanted to collect actual data to figure out how I feel in those situations. 

I became mindful of connections that I would not have otherwise noticed after tracking my feelings from Monday through Thursday. The most noticeable trend is that when I pick up my phone, I frequently feel exhausted. This feeling showed up every day, indicating that I don't always use my phone because I'm interested or involved, but occasionally because I'm exhausted or trying to find a quick way out. In addition, the concept of habit also appeared several times, particularly on Monday and Thursday. This helped me realize that picking up my phone is often a natural reaction rather than a conscious decision.

The combination of good and negative emotions occurring at the same time was another interesting pattern. For instance, I experienced both excitement and stress or boredom and distraction on Tuesday and Wednesday. This demonstrates how complicated my connection is with my phone. At the moment, it can make me feel happy, but it can also make me feel distracted or overwhelmed. The existence of FOMO (fear of missing out) on Wednesday was particularly significant since it illustrates the pressure my phone may put on me to be current and connected.

These patterns became much more clear when the data was shown using drawings and emojis. I was able to understand how often my phone is connected to negative or passive feelings rather than active, meaningful ones by observing repeated tired faces and distracted expressions. I would have assumed that I primarily use my phone for fun or interest if I hadn't kept track of this.

All things considered, this information has made me more mindful of my routines and feelings. It demonstrates that using a phone is more about how I feel at various times than it is about the device itself. This realization might encourage me to be more careful going forward and consider why I go for my phone.
